Egg omelette with shallots, and black pepper

The addition of finely chopped shallots and the kick of black pepper give this classic dish a flavourful edge. The key is to keep it simple, ensuring the eggs are fluffy and the ingredients are cooked just right.

Ingredients

  • Eggs 2 large
  • 1 tablespoon Shallots or onion, finely minced
  • Black pepper powder 1/8 teaspoon, or to taste
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh spinach 1 cup, roughly chopped(optional)

Kitchen-tools

  • Frying pan
  • Cutting board, knife
  • Bowl
  • Measuring spoons
  • Spatula
  • Fork or whisk

Instructions

  • Combine the finely minced shallots/onion, pepper powder, and salt. Rub the ingredients slightly with a fork to release their aromas. Crack the eggs into this mixture. Using a fork or whisk, beat the mixture well until everything is thoroughly combined and slightly frothy.
  • Place a pan over medium heat. Add oil and swirl the pan to ensure the oil coats the surface evenly. Pour the egg mixture into the hot pan.
  • Let the egg cook for a couple of minutes until the top is mostly firm. Gently lift one edge with a spatula and carefully flip the omelette over. Cook the second side for a few seconds; avoid overcooking to keep it soft.
  • If using the optional pre-sautéed spinach (or any other fillings you may have), sprinkle them evenly over one half of the omelette surface. Gently slide the spatula underneath the empty half of the omelette. Fold it over the filled side to create a half-moon shape. Cook for another minute or two on low heat until the center is cooked.
  • Slide the finished omelette onto a plate and serve.

Date, nut, and seed energy balls

Date, nut, and seed balls are a healthy snack that can be easily made at home using a blender. They are naturally sweetened by the dates and packed with fiber and protein.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup almonds
  • ½ cup cashews
  • ¼ cup peanuts
  • ½ cup pitted dates, chopped
  • ¼ cup mixed seeds (e.g., pumpkin, sunflower, sesame), divided
  • 1 tsp butter (optional)

Kitchen-tools

  • Blender/Food processor
  • pan
  • spatula
  • mixing bowls (2)
  • measuring cups

Instructions

  • Dry roast the mixed nuts and seeds for 2-3 minutes in a pan over medium heat. Remove them from the pan and let them cool completely.
  • Blend the cooled nuts and seeds first in a blender until they are a coarse, crumbly mixture, then transfer them to a bowl.
  • Blend the chopped dates until they form a coarse, sticky paste. Add the date paste in a large bowl and mash it with a spatula for a few minutes.
  • Add nuts and seeds mixture to the date mixture in the bowl. Mix everything thoroughly until well combined.
  • Once the mixture is cool enough to handle but still warm, lightly grease your palms with a little butter. Take small portions of the mixture and roll them into compact, round balls.
